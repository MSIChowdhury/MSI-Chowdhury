About Me

As an aspiring graduate student in Robotics and Mechatronics Engineering, I am deeply passionate about automated and industrial systems. My experience spans a wide array of sensors, actuation systems, microcontrollers, and single-board computers, with a particular focus on IoT-based systems.

My expertise lies in interfacing water quality sensors, specifically for measuring Electrical Conductivity (EC) and pH, skills I honed during my final year project. I am also keenly interested in Industrial IoT (IIoT) and its transformative potential in industrial automation.

With hands-on experience in Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) and a strong foundation in technical writing, I am well-equipped to contribute to cutting-edge research and development in robotics and automation. My goal is to pursue graduate studies to further advance my knowledge and make meaningful contributions to the field.

Projects

IoT-enabled Scalable Closed Hydroponics System with Smart Parameter Control

Undergraduate Thesis Project | January 2023 - January 2024

  • Designed and implemented an IoT-based scalable closed hydroponics system.
  • Monitored and controlled physical parameters required for plant growth (pH, Electrical Conductivity, CO2, Humidity, Air Temperature, Water Temperature).
  • Utilized Arduino Nano and Arduino Mega as nodes to handle sensor and actuator data separately.
  • Implemented an XGBoost-based classification method for independent control actions.
  • Developed a web-based dashboard for real-time monitoring with data visualizations and live video feed.
  • Project fully funded by the ICT Division under the ICT Innovation Fund.
  • Currently under the first round of reviews at the IEEE Journal of Internet of Things (IoT-J).

Relevance to Graduate Studies: This project demonstrates my ability to design and implement complex IoT systems, integrate multiple sensors and actuators, and apply machine learning techniques to real-world problems in agriculture and automation.

2 DOF, Single Claw Crawler Bot using Q-learning

Self-funded Lab Project | October 2022 - November 2022

  • Designed and built a 2 Degree-of-Freedom (DOF), single-claw crawler robot.
  • Implemented Q-learning to determine the best sequence of servo angles for maximum displacement.
  • Utilized epsilon-greedy algorithm to ensure faster convergence of Q-learning.

Relevance to Graduate Studies: This project showcases my skills in robotics design, reinforcement learning, and algorithm implementation, which are crucial for advanced research in autonomous robotic systems.
